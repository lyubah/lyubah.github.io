About Me

"Aren't you too social to be a master's student in computer science?" they often ask. To which I reply in a (bad) British accent, "You mustn't have met the lot of us."

I’m all about breaking coder stereotypes, blending high-energy social skills with a passion for machine learning, data science, and mathematics. I started with a strong foundation in mathematics and data science at UC Berkeley, which naturally evolved into a focus on cutting-edge machine learning and AI research during my master's in computer science at Washington State University.

When I’m not TAing or building neural networks, you’ll find me outdoors—skiing, climbing, and chasing sunsets. I’ve also dabbled in debugging code in the wild, but the jury’s still out on whether that’s a good idea. Currently, I’m writing my master’s thesis while training for my first half marathon.

GANs for Activity Recognition Survey

This paper explores the use of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to improve the robustness and accuracy of human activity recognition systems. By generating synthetic datasets that mimic real-world scenarios, the research showcases how GANs can address data scarcity and imbalances in traditional datasets. The work includes experiments and evaluations that demonstrate significant improvements in performance metrics.

Explainable AI in Complex Systems

A comparative analysis focusing on SHAP and LIME frameworks applied to Logistic Regression and XGBoost models. This study delves into the interpretability of AI models, emphasizing the importance of transparency in decision-making processes. The results highlight which methods are better suited for different types of machine learning tasks, along with actionable recommendations for practitioners.

Context-Tree-Based Algorithm for Stock Prediction

This research presents a novel probabilistic context-tree algorithm designed for predicting stock movements and measuring structural similarities in stock data. The method leverages temporal dependencies and hierarchical patterns to improve forecasting accuracy. Results demonstrate its efficacy compared to standard baseline methods in financial modeling.

Survey on SuperGANs for Privacy Applications

This survey investigates the use of SuperGANs to create synthetic datasets that enhance privacy while maintaining data utility. The paper covers applications in healthcare, IoT, and other sensitive domains where privacy concerns limit data sharing. It provides an in-depth analysis of SuperGAN architectures, evaluation metrics, and potential real-world implementations.
