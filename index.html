About Me

"Aren't you too social to be a master's student in computer science?" they often ask. To which I reply in a (bad) British accent, "You mustn't have met the lot of us."

I’m all about breaking coder stereotypes, blending high-energy social skills with a passion for machine learning, data science, and mathematics. I started with a strong foundation in mathematics and data science at UC Berkeley, which naturally evolved into a focus on cutting-edge machine learning and AI research during my master's in computer science at Washington State University.

When I’m not TAing or building neural networks, you’ll find me outdoors—skiing, climbing, and chasing sunsets. I’ve also dabbled in debugging code in the wild, but the jury’s still out on whether that’s a good idea. Currently, I’m writing my master’s thesis while training for my first half marathon.

GANs for Activity Recognition Survey

This paper explores the use of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to generate synthetic datasets for human activity recognition tasks. By balancing data scarcity issues, it demonstrates how GANs can improve the accuracy of recognition models, particularly for underrepresented activity classes. This research highlights practical applications in wearable technology and health monitoring systems.

Comparative Analysis of Explainable AI Frameworks

A comparative analysis of SHAP and LIME frameworks applied to Logistic Regression and XGBoost models. This research focuses on how interpretability can improve trust in AI systems, especially in high-stakes environments like healthcare and finance. The study provides insights on selecting explainability techniques based on model complexity.

Context-Tree-Based Algorithm for Stock Prediction

This paper introduces a context-tree-based algorithm to analyze temporal dependencies in stock market data. By capturing hierarchical patterns, this method enhances prediction accuracy and provides a novel approach to measuring structural similarities between stocks. Applications include portfolio optimization and risk analysis.

Survey on SuperGANs for Privacy Applications

This survey paper evaluates the effectiveness of SuperGANs in generating synthetic datasets that preserve privacy. It highlights applications in healthcare and IoT devices, ensuring compliance with data regulations while maintaining data utility. The paper also provides insights into the trade-offs between privacy and performance, making it a valuable resource for privacy-centric machine learning applications.
